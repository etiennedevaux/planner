Get Started

Remember

It is the pilot's responsibility to ensure that all flights are safe and legal. If in doubt do not fly.

Step OneSelect your competencies, skills and certifications.

Competencies

Ensure that you have read the manual so that you are familiar with the drone, its specifications, its capabilities and its limitations.

Ensure that you have obtained an operator ID from the Civil Aviation Authority.

Ensure that you have taken the online test and obtained a Flyer ID from the Civil Aviation Authority.

Ensure that you hold a current A2 Certificate of Competency from a CAA approved training organisation.

Ensure that you hold a current General Visual Line of Sight Certificate or GVC for short.

Step TwoSelect the drone category. Refer to the CAA CAP 722 for guidance.

Drone Categories

A drone weighing 250g or less, that is either a toy or has no camera or recording device.

A drone weighing 250g or less, that has a camera or other data recording device.

A drone that has been certified by the manufacturer as C1.

A drone that weighs below 500g.

A drone that is C2 certified, operating in low speed mode.

A drone that is C2 certified.

A drone that is below 2kg.

A drone that is over 2kg.

A drone that is over 2kg and has no built-in flight controls. Typically a model aircraft.

Flight Categories

A1 Fly Over - Stay Over

No miniumum separation from uninvolved people, potentially flying over, and staying over uninvolved people.

A1 Fly Over - Pass Over

No miniumum separation from uninvolved people, potentially flying over, but only passing briefly over uninvolved people.

A2 Close to People - 5m

Minimum separation of 5m from uninvolved people.

A2 Close to People - 30m

Minimum separation of 30m from uninvolved people.

A2 Close to People - 50m

Minimum separation of 50m from uninvolved people.

A3 Far From People

Minimum separation of 50m from uninvolved people AND you must remain over 150m from congested areas.
